GMAT and GRE


GMAT and GRE are standardized tests primarily used for admission to graduate-level programs.

Graduate Management Admission Test
Focus: Business-related programs such as MBA, Master's in Finance, and Master's in Management.
Sections: Analytical Writing Assessment, Integrated Reasoning, Quantitative, and Verbal Reasoning.
Graduate Record Examinations
Focus: A broader range of graduate programs including Master's and Ph.D. programs in various fields like engineering, humanities, and social sciences.
Sections: Analytical Writing, Verbal Reasoning, and Quantitative Reasoning.

Opportunities for FGLI students

Standardized tests can provide a common metric for evaluating students from diverse backgrounds. By excelling in these exams, students can demonstrate their academic abilities irrespective of their socioeconomic status.

Leveling the Playing
Field

Top-tier graduate programs often require high GMAT or GRE scores. These programs offer excellent education, networking opportunities, and career prospects.

Access to Prestigious
Programs

Many universities offer scholarships and financial aid based on academic merit, including test scores. This can significantly reduce the financial burden of graduate school.

Financial Aid and
Scholarships

Resources for preparation

Know the test pattern, syllabus and access free resources to learn.

GRE

ETS Official Website: Offers free practice tests, sample questions, and study materials.
GRE Prep Club: Provides a forum for test-takers to discuss questions and strategies.
Magazines: Publications like The Economist, The New Yorker, Forbes, and The Atlantic can help improve reading comprehension.
Online Tutorials: Many websites offer free GRE preparation tutorials and tips and comprehensive tutorials are available on youtube.

GMAT

GMAT Official Guide: Offers free practice tests, sample questions, and study materials.
GMAT Club: Provides a forum for test-takers to discuss questions and strategies.
Online Quant and Verbal Practice: Numerous websites offer free practice questions for both quantitative and verbal sections.
Business Magazines: Publications like Harvard Business Review, Forbes, and The Economist can help improve reading comprehension and critical thinking
