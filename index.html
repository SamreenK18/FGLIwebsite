Are you the first in your family to attend college? Navigating higher education can be challenging, but you don't have to do it alone. Welcome to Student Connect. Here, you'll find support, resources, and inspiration from fellow first-generation students who understand your journey. Connect with peers, share experiences, and access tools to help you succeed.

About FGLI

Demographics

Disproportionately from marginalized groups: They are often from underrepresented minority groups.
Higher likelihood of being female: Women from low-income backgrounds are more likely to be first-generation college students.
Older than peers: They tend to be older than their continuing-generation counterparts.


Some facts about FGLI students

Challenges

Financial constraints: Many face significant financial burdens, including tuition, fees, and living expenses.
Limited support network: Lack of family or friends with college experience can lead to feelings of
Balancing work and school: Many work to support themselves or their families, making it difficult to focus on studies.


Some facts about FGLI students

Outcomes

Lower graduation rates: Compared to their continuing-generation peers, they have lower college completion rates.
Higher student loan debt: They are more likely to accumulate student loan debt.
Lower post-graduation earnings: First-generation college graduates often earn less than their peers whose parents attended college.
